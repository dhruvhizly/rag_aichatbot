SECTION 3: WHAT IS THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ARI Defence Simulation Suite (also referred to as the Naval Simulation Suite)
is a collection of sophisticated training platforms each providing a complete
learning experience for specific naval warfare operations. It is considered one
of the most powerful naval simulation platforms available.

The suite is designed for armed forces and smaller response units for use in
training, tactical proving, mission rehearsal, and other defence-related
applications.

The simulators are designed and built to deliver a high degree of integration
and interoperability, enabling the practice of missions involving multiple force
elements performing in their own individual roles towards a common objective.

Each simulator can be operated in an individual mode, and multiple simulators
can be combined into different configurations to create:
- Team training opportunities
- Whole-ship training platforms
- Multiple collective whole-ship training platforms

The suite includes the following primary simulator categories:

WARSHIP BRIDGE AND NAVIGATION:
1. Bridge Operations Simulator (Primary Warship Bridge)
2. Secondary Warship Bridge Simulator
3. ECDIS and RADAR/ARPA Room

SURFACE WARFARE:

SECTION 4: WARSHIP BRIDGE AND NAVIGATION SIMULATORS — DETAILED OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 BRIDGE OPERATIONS SIMULATOR

The ARI vessel bridge simulator completely recreates the environment of a naval
vessel bridge down to the last detail. Combined with state-of-the-art
visualisation systems and capable of being mounted on a motion platform, these
simulators offer a completely realistic training environment for ship, convoy,
and fleet operations management.

Key characteristics:
- Full recreation of naval vessel bridge environment
- State-of-the-art visualisation systems
- Motion platform capability
- Suitable for ship, convoy, and fleet operations management training

Navigation and vessel management systems that can be populated include:
- Civilian band Radars
- GPS and navigation tools
- Military band Radars and position referencing systems
- Specific EWS (Electronic Warfare Support) scanning and jamming systems
- Weapon control systems
- Encrypted communications systems
- Subsea threat location tools

Customisation:
- Completely customised solutions available based on specific vessels including
  their specific performance and handling footprint
- Multifunctional: many different vessel types can be loaded into a single bridge
  simulator, allowing for training missions across all ship types

Integration capability:
- Can be connected to propulsion and machinery control simulators
- Can be connected to Command and Control (C&C) simulators
- Can be connected to weapon control and intelligence systems simulators
- Can be connected to other bridge simulators for:
  * Whole-ship training
  * Convoy management
  * Fleet management training

Training scenarios supported:
- Convoy and escort operations
- Tactical maneuvering
- Piracy response

4.2 ECDIS SIMULATOR (DEFENCE)

The ARI Electronic Chart Display and Information System (ECDIS) Simulator is
a comprehensive training solution for electronic navigation.

Key characteristics:
- Complies with the relevant requirements of latest STCW amendments
  (2010 Manila Amendments), under sections A-I/12, A-II/1, and A-II/2
- Fulfils requirements of IMO Model Course 1.27 related to the use of simulation
- Can be operated in standalone training mode or in conjunction with other
  navigation aids and equipment
- Provides training in ECDIS-based navigation including use of electronic charts,
  route planning, and route monitoring
- Can be used for competence demonstration in supervised or unsupervised modes
- Multiple OEM ECDIS, fully integrated with Navigation Simulators, available

4.3 RADAR / ARPA SIMULATOR (DEFENCE)

The ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator provides IMO-compliant training in radar navigation
and automatic radar plotting aid operation.

Key characteristics:
- Built to comply with IMO performance standards for Radar equipment on
  seagoing vessels
- Designed to meet requirements of IMO Model Courses 1.07 and 1.08
- Suitable for competence training and assessment in radar operation in
  accordance with STCW 2010 simulator-based training requirements
- Available with real OEM equipment or emulated Radars

4.4 GMDSS AND COMMUNICATIONS SIMULATOR (DEFENCE)

The ARI Communications Simulator provides complete operational training in
communication using equipment as found on sea-going vessels.

Key characteristics:
- Complies with Performance Standards for Simulators as defined by STCW
- Approved by DNV to the highest Class A Standard
- Specific configurations are AMERC approved
- Provides an operating environment for the trainee similar to operating a
  communications station on a modern ship
- Suitable for training personnel at all levels for handling distress and
  routine communication at sea using satellite and terrestrial communication
  equipment

SECTION 5: SURFACE WARFARE SIMULATORS — DETAILED OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 FAST ATTACK CRAFT (FAC) SIMULATOR

The FAC Simulator is a high-fidelity, fully integrated training solution designed
to replicate the operational environment of modern Fast Attack Crafts. Engineered
for realism, safety, and repeatability, the simulator provides naval operators
and combat crews with immersive training in ship handling, combat tactics,
navigation, and remote weapon engagement.

Key characteristics:
- High-Fidelity Visualization: Immersive 360-degree horizontal field of view
  (HFOV) environment with dynamic maritime scenario rendering for enhanced
  awareness and tactical training
- Integrated Human-Machine Interface (HMI): Realistic vessel control interface
  with propulsion and steering systems for authentic operator interaction
- Weapon System Simulation: Optional integration with remotely operated
  stabilised weapon station for precise targeting
- Medium Machine Gun (MMG) simulation with recoil kit for realistic weapon
  training, covering target engagement, fire control, and tactical scenarios
- 6-DOF Motion Platform: Synchronised six-axis motion system aligned with
  navigation and weapons modules for a fully immersive training experience

5.2 ACTION SPEED TACTICAL TRAINER (ASTT)

The ASTT delivers immersive, high-fidelity naval training for ships, submarines,
and aircraft command teams. It enables forces to practice, refine, and test
tactics across domains including Anti-Submarine, Anti-Air, Mine, Amphibious,
and Electronic Warfare — enhancing readiness, coordination, and combat
effectiveness in a true-to-mission environment.

Supported warfare domains:
- Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW)
- Anti-Air Warfare (AAW)
- Surface Warfare
- Mine Warfare
- Amphibious Warfare
- Electronic Warfare (EW)

Additional capabilities:
- Data and voice communications
- Air operations (ship, carrier, and land-based)
- Sonobuoy monitoring
- Automatic Identification System (AIS) / Air Data System (ADS) /
  Identification Friend or Foe (IFF) tracking
- Replenishment and logistics
- Real-time combat situation simulation including situation assessment,
  information analysis, command decision-making, and reporting

Realistic Maritime Environment features include:
- Authentic 3D maritime environment with accurate coastlines, depth contours,
  and seabed profiles
- Depth below keel indication, chart-based navigation, and grounding detection
- Realistic platform dynamics with sensor modelling based on physics, radar,
  sonar, and environmental equations
- Advanced systems including:
  * Radar
  * Sonar (Active / Passive)
  * Electronic Support Measures (ESM)
  * Communication Intercept
  * Sonobuoy
  * Variable Depth Sonar (VDS)
  * Magnetic Anomaly Detector (MAD)
  * Visual Sensors
- Weapon and countermeasure modelling including:
  * Torpedoes
  * Missiles
  * Guns
  * Bombs
  * Electronic Counter Measures (ECM)
  * Decoys (chaff and jamming)
- Environmental and hydrological effects simulation
- Damage assessment and battle damage survivability
- Data and voice communication, logistics and replenishment operations
- Automated platform-level actions (mine / sonobuoy deployment, route search
  patterns)
- Dedicated sensor displays and real-time decision-making tools
- Comprehensive briefing and debriefing facilities

9.1 HELICOPTER CONTROL SIMULATION

The Helicopter Control Simulation module is a high-fidelity naval aviation
training system designed to support ship-helicopter coordination and operations

The system allows crews to rehearse time-critical and multi-asset naval missions,

enhances mission readiness, operational safety, and crew coordination without
the risks and costs of live flying.

Key capabilities:
- Training for helicopter controllers and aircrew coordination
- Simulation of integrated ship-helicopter operations and deck procedures
- Support for Ship Controlled Approach (SCA), Search and Rescue (SAR),
  and maritime search planning
- Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW), weapons delivery, and reconnaissance mission
  scenarios
- High-fidelity mission environments for safe, repeatable, and effective training

SECTION 12: JOINT MILITARY OPERATIONS AND INTEROPERABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARI uses advanced technological tools including Common Databases and High Level
Architecture (HLA) systems to build interoperability into simulation systems.
Multiple simulators and simulator types can be interfaced to permit platform,
fleet, and force-level training scenarios to be executed.

Recording and Debriefing:
- Simulators have the ability to record complete training sessions including:
  * Equipment operation sequences
  * Visual images
  * Sensor data
  * CCTV views of the simulator rooms
  * Voice communications
- All recording devices are fully synchronised and time-stamped
- All recordings can be replayed at any time, including immediately after a
  live session for debrief or evaluation
- Recordings can be replayed at variable speeds
- Can be interrupted to resume a live session at any point from within the
  recording

Network and Interoperability:
- Interoperability between simulator systems for diverse forces is possible
  through the use of common database systems and common industry standard
  communication and storage protocols
- Distributed simulation infrastructure is fully compatible with operations
  across secured defence-grade networks

Exercise Management Systems:
- Every ARI simulator is equipped with advanced exercise management systems
  including tools for:
  * Exercise creation and design
  * Object modelling
  * Environment modelling
  * Weather models
  * Assessment and evaluation
  * Communications
  * Recording and debriefing
- Relevant simulation training can be integrated with respective service
  training curricula to provide immersive, participatory, and repeatable
  learning experiences

complete learning experience for specific naval warfare operations. It is
considered one of the most powerful naval simulation platforms available.
Products are built to military standards and used by multiple armed forces
worldwide.

Q: What types of defence simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI offers simulators for warship bridge operations, fast attack craft,
action speed tactical training, combat information centres, submarine operations,
deep submergence rescue vehicles, warship machinery, helicopter control, domain
awareness and coastal security, cyber warfare, and mixed reality training. These
can operate individually or be integrated into a unified simulation complex.

Q: Can ARI defence simulators be networked together?
A: Yes. ARI uses Common Databases and High Level Architecture (HLA) systems
to achieve interoperability between simulators. Multiple simulators and types
can be interfaced to permit platform, fleet, and force-level training scenarios.
The distributed simulation infrastructure is fully compatible with secured
defence-grade networks.

Q: What is the ARI Integrated Naval Simulation Complex?
A: It is a cutting-edge training environment that seamlessly integrates multiple
high-fidelity simulators into a unified ecosystem, enabling joint training
across naval, air, land, and subsurface forces. It can include the Primary and
Secondary Warship Bridge Simulators, FAC Simulator, Submarine Simulator, DSRV
Simulator, CIC Simulator, ASTT, Helicopter Control Simulator, Coastal Security
Solution, and a Unified Simulation Control Centre, among others.

Q: What is the Action Speed Tactical Trainer (ASTT)?
A: The ASTT delivers immersive, high-fidelity naval training for ships,
submarines, and aircraft command teams. It supports training in Anti-Submarine
Warfare (ASW), Anti-Air Warfare (AAW), Surface Warfare, Mine Warfare, Amphibious
Warfare, and Electronic Warfare (EW), with a realistic 3D maritime environment,
advanced sensor modelling, and comprehensive weapon and countermeasure simulation.

Q: What is the Combat Information Centre (CIC) Simulator?
A: The CIC Simulator is purpose-built for warfare training centres and active-duty
surface combat units. It provides an immersive environment that replicates modern
naval combat complexity, featuring Surface Radar Modules, a Sonar Module, an
Electronic Warfare Suite, and a Fire Control System (FCS) for training in threat
detection, targeting, and weapon engagement.

Q: What does the Submarine Operations and Navigation Simulator include?
A: It is a high-fidelity training platform replicating complex submarine mission
dynamics. It includes Navigation Systems, Control Interfaces, Operations Modules,
a Platform Management System (PMS), Position Plotting System, Periscope
Simulation, Scenario-Based Training, Environmental Simulation, Crew Coordination
tools, and Performance Feedback capabilities.

Q: What is the DSRV Simulator used for?
A: The DSRV (Deep Submergence Rescue Vehicle) Simulator trains naval and maritime
personnel in deploying, manoeuvring, and recovering a DSRV in challenging
deep-sea rescue situations. It uses high-fidelity hydrodynamic modelling and
immersive visualisation, and includes SRV and LARS (Launch and Recovery System)
stations as well as an ROV Station.

Q: What warfare domains does the Helicopter Control Simulation support?
A: It supports training for helicopter controllers and aircrew coordination,
integrated ship-helicopter operations, deck procedures, Ship Controlled Approach
(SCA), Search and Rescue (SAR), maritime search planning, Anti-Submarine Warfare
(ASW), weapons delivery, and reconnaissance missions.

Q: What is the Domain Awareness and Coastal Security Solution?
A: It is a unified, real-time naval surveillance and command solution providing
comprehensive awareness across maritime, air, subsurface, cyber, and space
domains. It fuses data from coastal sensors, naval platforms, tracking systems,
and intelligence sources to generate a single integrated operational picture,
enabling early threat detection, faster decision-making, and 24x7 monitoring.

Q: What does the ARI Cyber Warfare Simulator cover?
A: The Cyber Warfare Simulator models cyber threats, vulnerabilities, and
defensive operations across naval networks, platforms, and combat systems. It
simulates cyber-attacks on Radar, Navigation, Sonar, Electronic Warfare,
Communications, Vessel Control Systems, Combat and Weapon Control Systems,
and Vessel Port Infrastructure, supporting operational readiness and strategic
planning in contested cyber maritime environments.

Q: Does ARI offer Mixed Reality solutions for defence?
A: Yes. ARI's Mixed Reality (MR) solutions integrate physical and digital
environments for ultra-realistic, mission-ready simulations. They cover Combat
and Tactical Training (battlefield scenarios, urban warfare, diverse terrain)
and Naval Operations Simulation (ship manoeuvring, combat drills, emergency
response, anti-piracy operations).

Q: What is the Fast Attack Craft (FAC) Simulator?
A: The FAC Simulator is a high-fidelity, fully integrated training solution
replicating the operational environment of modern Fast Attack Crafts. It features
a 360-degree HFOV immersive visualisation environment, an integrated HMI with
propulsion and steering systems, optional weapon station integration with Medium
Machine Gun (MMG) simulation and a recoil kit, and a 6-DOF motion platform.

Q: How does ARI ensure data security and confidentiality in defence simulation?
A: ARI has been audited and certified to ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 standards, which
include assessment of confidential data protection systems. ARI also holds ISO
27001 (Information Security Management) and ISO 45001 (Occupational Health and
Safety) certifications. The distributed simulation infrastructure operates
across secured defence-grade networks.
