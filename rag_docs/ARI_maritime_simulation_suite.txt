SECTION 9: FEASIBILITY STUDIES — SAFEPORT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Simulation studies for vessel navigation and manoeuvring are an integral part
of any port development project — be it a Greenfield or Brownfield port, harbour,
berth, river, terminal, or inland waterway.

The ARI SAFEPORT programme is designed to study the feasibility of safe
navigational operation of the intended vessel type(s) in the proposed manoeuvring
area before large resources are committed to the development of waterways and
port infrastructure.

SAFEPORT analyses include:
- Ability of vessels to manoeuvre in the approach channel / turning circle in a
  safe, timely, and efficient manner under the combined effects of current, wind,
  tide, and swell
- Ability of vessels to arrive at and depart from proposed berths
- Confirming the size, power, and number of tugs needed to assist vessels during
  transit, turning, berthing, and unberthing, including the ability to assist
  during emergency conditions
- Ability of river boats, including barge convoys, to negotiate the bends and
  turns of river sections including entering and leaving lock areas, if required

SECTION 13: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is ARI Simulation and who owns it?
A: Applied Research International (ARI) Simulation is the naval and marine
simulation arm of Zen Technologies Limited. It is a global leader in simulation
and virtual reality training solutions for the defence, marine, and offshore
industries. Its tagline is "YOUR WORLD — OUR WORLD."

Q: What certification standard does the entire ARI marine and offshore simulator
range hold?
A: The entire ARI marine and offshore simulation range is certified by DNV
(Det Norske Veritas) to Class A Standard — the highest classification awarded
for maritime training simulators. Individual products also hold additional
certifications relevant to their specific application (STCW, AMERC, IALA, NI,
IMCA, etc.).

Q: What are the three main product suites ARI offers?
A: ARI's portfolio is organised into three primary suites: the Marine and
Offshore Suite (over 20 types of marine and offshore simulators, over 30 types
of crane simulators, ports and terminals), the Defence Suite (simulators for
a range of weapon systems, air assets, and naval platforms), and Ports and
Terminals (Crane and HEMM simulators). The entire marine and offshore range
is DNV certified.

Q: What navigation simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI's navigation simulator range includes the Full Mission Ship Handling /
Navigation Simulator, Tug Handling Simulator, VTS Simulator, GMDSS/Communications
Simulator, RADAR/ARPA Simulator, and ECDIS Simulator — all approved to IMO, STCW
2010, and DNV Class A standards where applicable.

Q: What offshore simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI's offshore simulation suite includes the Full Mission Offshore Bridge
Simulator, Dynamic Positioning (DP) Simulator (NI and DNV Class A certified),
Anchor Handling Simulator, Offshore Machinery Simulator, and Subsea/ROV
Simulator (DNV Class A, IMCA compliant).

Q: What does the Full Mission Engine Room Simulator include?
A: It trains Marine Engineers at Watch-Keeping, Operational, and Management
levels. It models multiple propulsion types including slow, medium, and
high-speed diesel, electric, azimuth, and waterjet engines, and includes new
green fuel and decarbonisation engine models (LNG, methanol, dual fuel), plus
emission control systems (HP SCR, LP SCR, EGR).

Q: What is the Liquid Cargo Handling Simulator?
A: It simulates the real-time process flow control systems used in the storage
and transfer of potentially hazardous bulk liquids from shore to ship and vice
versa. It covers Oil Tankers, VLCCs, Product Tankers, Chemical Tankers, LPG
Carriers, and LNG Carriers, and is scalable from a single PC solution to a full
mission configuration. It is considered among the most versatile training tools
of its kind in the world.

Q: What types of crane simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI offers over 30 types of crane simulators including QC (Ship to Shore),
RTG, RMG, Straddle Carrier, Mobile Harbour Crane, Offshore Pedestal Crane,
Knuckle Boom Crane, Forklift, and many others. Solutions range from desktop
editions to cabin-based full mission editions with motion systems.

Q: What are ARI's HEMM simulators?
A: ARI's Heavy Earth Moving Machinery (HEMM) Simulators provide high-quality
training for mining operations personnel on equipment including Tippers, Dozers,
Dumpers, Wheel Loaders, LHDs, Shovels, Backhoes, and Excavators.

Q: What defence simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI's defence suite includes Naval Bridge Operations Simulators, Fast Attack
Craft (FAC) Simulator, Action Speed Tactical Trainer (ASTT), Combat Information
Centre (CIC) Simulator, Submarine Operations and Navigation Simulator, DSRV
Simulator, Warship Machinery Simulator, Helicopter Control Simulator, and Domain
Awareness and Coastal Security Solution. These can operate individually or be
integrated into a multi-simulator Naval Simulation Complex.

Q: Does ARI offer cloud-based simulation training?
A: Yes. All versions of simulators in ARI's portfolio are available through
ARI's Cloud Based Simulation Training platform. The marine range of cloud modules
is DNV Approved. ARI is among only three globally certified companies approved
by both IADC and IWCF for cloud-based drilling and well control assessment.
More than 75,000 certificates have been generated using ARI cloud systems.

Q: What is the ASBA platform?
A: ASBA (Automated Scenario/Simulation Based Assessment) is ARI's digital
assessment platform that sets the standards for digital assessment in the
maritime industry. It features adaptive scenario-based question banks, engagement
monitoring, dashboard analysis, immediate results, secure scoring, and multiple
proctoring options for training, screening, assessment, and competency development.

Q: Do ARI simulators support remote and hybrid training?
A: Yes. ARI simulators support hybrid and blended learning, enabling remote
trainees to fully engage in training alongside on-site participants. Remote
participants can access video, RADAR, ECDIS, and other simulator functions, with
instructors able to monitor all participants simultaneously.

Q: What is VR/AR/MR training from ARI?
A: ARI's Mixed Reality (MR) solutions provide wholly immersive training
environments. The complete VR solution package includes wireless VR glasses
(HMD), wireless handheld controllers, and custom VR simulation software per
training module. Trainees can virtually move through machinery spaces and perform
operations such as opening valves, turning knobs, pressing buttons, and moving
levers.

Q: What is the SAFEPORT programme?
A: SAFEPORT is ARI's simulation-based port and waterway feasibility study
programme. It analyses the safety and feasibility of vessel navigation and
manoeuvring in proposed or existing port, harbour, berth, river, and waterway
environments. It is used as part of Greenfield and Brownfield port development
projects to confirm safe operations before committing resources to infrastructure.
