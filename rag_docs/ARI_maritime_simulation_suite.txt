SECTION 2: COMPANY OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Applied Research International (ARI) is at the forefront of maritime simulation
innovation. Its simulators are meticulously crafted to adhere to the highest
international standards set by respected industry authorities including:

- IMO (International Maritime Organization)
- STCW 2010 (Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers)
- The Nautical Institute (NI)
- OPITO (Offshore Petroleum Industry Training Organisation)
- AMERC (Association of Marine Electronics and Radio Colleges)
- IMCA (International Marine Contractors Association)
- DNV (Det Norske Veritas)

ARI's marine, offshore, and crane simulation solutions have achieved DNV Class A
Standard certification — the highest level of simulator certification awarded by
Det Norske Veritas. ARI has also been audited and certified to ISO 14001 and
ISO 9001 standards, which include assessment of confidential data protection
systems.

ARI's product portfolio is organised into three primary suites:
- Marine & Offshore Suite (over 20 types of marine and offshore simulators)
- Defence Suite (simulators for armed forces across weapon systems, air assets,
  and naval platforms)
- Ports & Terminals Suite (over 30 types of crane simulators)

The entire marine and offshore simulation range is DNV certified.

Using ARI cloud systems, more than 75,000 certificates have been generated.
ARI is among only three globally certified simulation companies approved by
both IADC and IWCF for cloud-based assessment of drilling and well control

SECTION 4: NAVIGATION SIMULATORS — DETAILED OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The navigation simulators within the ARI Maritime Simulation Suite form the
core of the company's marine training offering. They are the primary tools
used to train navigational officers, VTS operators, and communications personnel
to the standards required by IMO, STCW 2010, and other internationally
recognised certification bodies.

All navigation simulators in the suite share the following characteristics:
- Designed, built, and installed in accordance with IMO and STCW 2010 requirements
- Approved by DNV to the highest Class A standards
- Available in multiple configurations from desktop/part-task trainers to
  large-scale full-mission installations
- Capable of standalone operation or integration into multi-simulator complexes
- Support both initial certification training and revalidation/refresher programmes

4.1 FULL MISSION SHIP HANDLING / NAVIGATION SIMULATOR

The ARI Full Mission Ship Manoeuvring Simulator is a complete maritime navigation
training solution for all training needs related to navigation and ship handling.

Key characteristics:
- Designed, built, and installed in accordance with IMO, STCW 2010 requirements
- Approved by DNV to Class A standard
- Includes RADAR-ARPA, ECDIS, and full navigational equipment suite
- Training conducted in an immersive atmosphere with full visual, instrument,
  and aural cues
- Available in configurations from tabletop and part-task trainers to large-scale
  installations with 360-degree field of view
- Supports specialised navigation operations including:
  * Integrated Bridge Systems (IBS)
  * Ice Navigation
  * Ship-to-Ship operations
  * Single Buoy Mooring (SBM) operations
  * VTIS (Vessel Traffic and Information Service) operations

4.2 ECDIS SIMULATOR

The ARI ECDIS Simulator is a comprehensive training solution for Electronic Chart
Display and Information System operations.

Key characteristics:
- Complies with the relevant requirements of STCW 2010 Manila Amendments
- Meets STCW sections A-I/12, A-II/1, and A-II/2
- Fulfils requirements of IMO Model Course 1.27 related to the use of simulation
- Trains officers in the use of ECDIS as a primary means of navigation

4.3 RADAR / ARPA SIMULATOR

The ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator provides IMO-compliant training in radar navigation
and automatic radar plotting aid operation.

Key characteristics:
- Complies with IMO performance standards for radar equipment on seagoing vessels
- Designed to meet IMO Model Courses 1.07 and 1.08
- Suitable for competence training and assessment in radar operation in accordance
  with STCW 2010 simulator-based training requirements
- Supports both operational level (1.07) and management level (1.08) training
- Available with real OEM radar equipment or high-fidelity emulated radar systems
- Includes competence assessment tools for evaluating operator proficiency

Full detail: see document radar-arpa-sim-001

4.4 TUG HANDLING SIMULATOR

The ARI Tug Handling Simulator provides a practical and realistic training
platform for tug operators at all levels.

Key characteristics:
- Available in multiple configuration types
- Closely resembles the layout and equipment of a modern tug manoeuvring station
  and navigation bridge
- Multiple tug types available including ASD (Azimuth Stern Drive) tugs
- Sophisticated behavioural model ensures complex tug manoeuvres are performed
  to a high degree of accuracy under various environmental and operational conditions
- Includes dedicated emergency manoeuvre and response scenario training

Full detail: see document tug-sim-001

4.5 VTS (VESSEL TRAFFIC SERVICE) SIMULATOR

The ARI VTS Simulator trains personnel in the management of vessel traffic
in port, harbour, and coastal environments.

Key characteristics:
- Designed, built, and implemented to meet and exceed IALA model courses V-103
- Provides a platform for consistent, quality training of VTS operating personnel
- Trains candidates to deal with real-world vessel traffic management situations
  under both normal and emergency conditions
- Scenarios can be pre-prepared to fulfil specific training objectives
- Scenario complexity is graded to meet the requirements of operators across
  a wide spread of individual experience levels

Full detail: see document vts-sim-001

4.6 GMDSS / COMMUNICATIONS SIMULATOR

The ARI GMDSS Simulator provides complete operational communications training
using GMDSS equipment as found on marine and offshore vessels.

Key characteristics:
- Complies with STCW 2010 performance standards for simulators as defined in
  Sections A-I/12 and B-I/12
- Approved by DNV to the highest Class A Standard
- Specific configurations are AMERC approved for official GMDSS certification
- Provides an operating environment similar to operating a GMDSS station on a
  modern ship
- Suitable for training navigators at all levels in handling distress and routine
  communications using satellite and terrestrial communication equipment

Full detail: see document gmdss-sim-001

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 FULL MISSION OFFSHORE BRIDGE SIMULATOR

The ARI Full Mission Offshore Bridge Simulator recreates the complete experience
of operating on the bridge of an offshore vessel.

Key characteristics:

- Extremely high level fidelity visual system
- Delivers an immersive virtual reality experience
- Broad range of offshore vessel types including:
  * PSV (Platform Supply Vessels)
  * AHTSV (Anchor Handling Tug Supply Vessels)
  * MPSV (Multi-Purpose Support Vessels)
  * MSV (Marine Support Vessels)
- Vessel models available with variety of control and propulsion systems including:
  * Conventional telegraphs
  * Azimuth controls
  * Z-drives
  * Thrusters
  * Joystick controls

7.2 DYNAMIC POSITIONING (DP) SIMULATOR

The ARI Dynamic Positioning Simulator provides a powerful platform for DP
training and certification.

Key characteristics:
- Fully complies with NI (Nautical Institute) and DNV Class A Standards
- Scalable product available as:
  * Standalone trainer in a classroom
  * Multi-station configuration
  * Integrated into the ARI Offshore Bridge Simulator for complete DP/DP-2

7.3 ANCHOR HANDLING SIMULATOR

The ARI Anchor Handling Simulator is an add-on module for the Offshore Bridge
Simulator providing complete anchor handling capabilities.

Key characteristics:
- Supports basic and advanced anchor handling operations
- Physics-based simulation of the complete anchor handling process including:
  * Transmission of anchor pattern, interpretation and planning
  * Picking up pennant wires and anchors
  * Deployment and retrieval of anchors in position
- Full set of winch controls operating in parallel with vessel handling controls
- Single and multiple vessel missions can be executed

7.4 SUBSEA / ROV SIMULATOR

The ARI ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle) Simulator is certified by DNV to
Class A Standard and meets IMCA guidelines for ROV simulator classes.

Key characteristics:
- Certified by DNV to the highest Class A Standard
- Meets requirements for Class A simulator according to IMCA guidelines
- Used by ROV manufacturers, operators, oil companies, and others for:
  * Field visualisation (as built)
  * Mission planning and dive plan development and evaluation
  * Subsea equipment design and testing prior to field deployment
  * Equipment-specific, mission-specific, and site-specific ROV pilot training
- Allows pilots and potential pilots to gain 'stick time' without risking
  equipment or tying up expensive field assets
- Deployed on networked PCs with distributed processing
- Supports multiple dynamic objects including two simultaneous ROV systems
- Supports large umbilical lengths for deep water operations
- Fully interactive manipulators

7.5 OFFSHORE MACHINERY SIMULATOR

The ARI Machinery Simulator recreates the propulsion and machinery plant of

Key characteristics:
- Comprehensive platform for training and assessment of operating personnel
- Simulates all machinery-related processes of an offshore vessel

- Supports offshore systems including:
  * FIFI (Fire Fighting) systems
  * Cargo handling systems
  * Hydraulic system controls
  * Complete power management system
- Entire machinery space available in 3D for use as a standalone familiarisation

SECTION 9: FEASIBILITY STUDIES — SAFEPORT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ARI SAFEPORT programme provides simulation-based port and waterway
feasibility studies.

SAFEPORT is used for:
- Greenfield and Brownfield port development projects
- Harbour, berth, river, terminal, and inland waterway development
- Studying the feasibility of safe navigational operation of intended vessel types
  in a proposed manoeuvring area before large resources are committed

SAFEPORT analyses include:
- Ability of vessels to manoeuvre in approach channels and turning circles
  safely under combined effects of current, wind, tide, and swell
- Ability of vessels to arrive at and depart from proposed berths
- Confirming the size, power, and number of tugs needed to assist vessels
  during transit, turning, berthing, and unberthing, including emergency conditions
- Ability of river boats and barge convoys to negotiate bends, turns, and
  lock areas in river sections

SECTION 13: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is ARI Simulation and who owns it?
A: Applied Research International (ARI) Simulation is the naval and marine
simulation arm of Zen Technologies Limited. It is a global leader in simulation
and virtual reality training solutions for the defence, marine, and offshore
industries. Its tagline is "YOUR WORLD — OUR WORLD."

Q: What certification standard does the entire ARI marine and offshore
simulator range hold?
A: The entire ARI marine and offshore simulation range is certified by DNV
(Det Norske Veritas) to Class A Standard — the highest classification awarded
for maritime training simulators. Individual products also hold additional
certifications relevant to their specific application (STCW, AMERC, IALA,
NI, IMCA, etc.).

Q: What simulators does ARI offer for navigation training?
A: ARI's navigation simulator range within the Maritime Simulation Suite includes
the Full Mission Ship Handling Simulator, ECDIS Simulator, RADAR/ARPA Simulator,
Tug Handling Simulator, VTS Simulator, and GMDSS/Communications Simulator.

Q: Can ARI simulators be integrated into a single training complex?
A: Yes. ARI's Multi-Simulator Simulation Complex integrates multiple simulators
into a unified ecosystem, enabling joint training exercises that connect bridge,
engine room, communications, VTS, and other simulators. Multiple simulator types
can be networked for team-based, mission-oriented training.

Q: Does ARI offer cloud-based simulation training?
A: Yes. All versions of simulators in ARI's portfolio are available through
ARI's Cloud Based Simulation Training platform. The marine range of cloud
modules is DNV Approved. ARI is among only three globally certified companies
approved by both IADC and IWCF for cloud-based drilling and well control
assessment. More than 75,000 certificates have been generated using ARI cloud
systems.

Q: Do ARI simulators support remote and hybrid training delivery?
A: Yes. ARI simulators support hybrid and blended learning, enabling remote
trainees to fully engage in training alongside on-site participants. Remote
participants can access video, RADAR, ECDIS, and other simulator functions,
with instructors able to monitor all participants simultaneously.

Q: What is the SAFEPORT programme?
A: SAFEPORT is ARI's simulation-based port feasibility study programme. It
analyses the safety and feasibility of vessel navigation and manoeuvring in
proposed or existing port, harbour, berth, river, and waterway environments.
It is used as part of port development projects to confirm safe operations
before committing large resources to infrastructure development.

Q: What offshore simulators does ARI offer?
A: ARI's offshore simulation suite includes the Full Mission Offshore Bridge
Simulator, Dynamic Positioning (DP) Simulator (NI and DNV Class A certified),
Anchor Handling Simulator, Offshore Machinery Simulator, and Subsea/ROV
Simulator (DNV Class A, IMCA compliant).

Q: Does ARI offer simulators for engine room training?
A: Yes. The ARI Full Mission Engine Room Simulator trains Marine Engineers at
Watch-Keeping, Operational, and Management levels. It models multiple propulsion
types including diesel, electric, azimuth, and waterjet engines, and includes
new green fuel and decarbonisation engine models such as LNG, methanol, and
dual-fuel configurations.

Q: What is ARI's ASBA platform?
A: ASBA (Automated Scenario/Simulation Based Assessment) is ARI's digital
assessment platform for the maritime industry. It features adaptive scenario-
based question banks, engagement monitoring, dashboard analysis, report
generation, and multiple proctoring options. It can be used for training,
screening, assessment, and competency development.
