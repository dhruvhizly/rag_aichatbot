SECTION 2: WHO IS ARI SIMULATION?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Applied Research International (ARI) Simulation, a subsidiary of Zen Technologies
Limited, is a globally recognised leader in the design, development, and delivery
of simulation and virtual reality training solutions. ARI serves two primary
industries: the defence sector (navies and armed forces worldwide) and the
commercial maritime, marine, and offshore sector.

ARI's simulators are built to the highest international standards, holding
certifications and approvals from a broad range of globally respected industry
and regulatory bodies including:

- IMO (International Maritime Organization)
- STCW 2010 (Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers)
- DNV (Det Norske Veritas) — Class A certification for the entire marine and
  offshore simulation range
- The Nautical Institute (NI)
- OPITO (Offshore Petroleum Industry Training Organisation)
- AMERC (Association of Marine Electronics and Radio Colleges)
- IMCA (International Marine Contractors Association)
- IALA (International Association of Marine Aids to Navigation)
- ISO 9001 (Quality Management)
- ISO 14001 (Environmental Management)

ARI's products are built to military standards for the defence sector and to the
highest civilian certification standards for the commercial maritime sector.

Global Reach:
- 60+ countries with installations
- 1,500+ installations worldwide
- Offices in India (Delhi and Mumbai), USA (California), and Singapore
- 55+ copyrights held in India, globally recognised
- More than 75,000 certificates generated through ARI cloud simulation systems

ARI offers two primary simulation suites:

1. THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE
   Covering marine, offshore, ports and terminals, cloud and digital technologies,
   and feasibility studies — for commercial maritime, offshore, and port operators,
   training institutions, and certification bodies.

2. THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE
   Covering naval warfare, submarine, aviation, cyber, coastal security, and
   integrated multi-simulator complexes — for navies, armed forces, and defence
   training establishments worldwide.

Both suites are described in detail in the sections below.

SECTION 3: SUITE 1 — THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 WHAT IS THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE?

The ARI Maritime Simulation Suite (also referred to as the Marine and Logistics
Simulation portfolio) covers the entire range of commercial marine, offshore,
and port operations. It is designed for maritime academies, training centres,
shipping companies, offshore operators, port authorities, and certification bodies.

The suite provides simulators for Bridge, Engine Room, Cargo, Communications,
Offshore, Subsea, Ports and Terminals, and Cloud-based training. All simulators
in the suite share the following fundamental characteristics:

- Designed, built, and installed in accordance with IMO and STCW 2010 requirements
- Approved by DNV to the highest Class A Standard
- Available in multiple configurations from desktop / part-task trainers to
  large-scale full-mission installations
- Capable of standalone operation or integration into multi-simulator complexes
- Support both initial certification training and revalidation / refresher
  programmes

3.2 WHAT DOES THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE INCLUDE?

The Maritime Simulation Suite is organised across four sub-categories:

MARINE SUITE (Navigation, Bridge, Engine, Cargo):
- Full Mission Ship Handling / Navigation Simulator — IMO, STCW 2010, DNV Class A
- Full Mission Tug Handling Simulator
- VTS (Vessel Traffic Service) Simulator — IALA V-103 compliant
- GMDSS / Communications Simulator — STCW 2010, DNV Class A, AMERC approved
- RADAR / ARPA Simulator — IMO Model Courses 1.07 and 1.08, STCW 2010
- ECDIS Simulator — STCW 2010, IMO Model Course 1.27
- Full Mission Engine Room Simulator — including Green Fuel / Decarbonisation
  engine models (LNG, Methanol, Dual Fuel)
- Liquid Cargo Handling / Process Simulator — Oil, Chemical, LPG, LNG tankers
- LNG and Methanol Bunkering Simulator

OFFSHORE AND SUBSEA SUITE:
- Full Mission Offshore Bridge Simulator — PSV, AHTSV, MPSV, MSV vessel types
- Dynamic Positioning (DP) Simulator — NI and DNV Class A certified
- Anchor Handling Simulator
- Offshore Machinery Simulator
- Subsea / ROV Simulator — DNV Class A, IMCA compliant

PORTS AND TERMINALS:
- Crane Simulators — over 30 types including QC (Ship to Shore), RTG, RMG,
  Straddle Carrier, Mobile Harbour Crane, Offshore Pedestal Crane, Knuckle
  Boom Crane, Forklift, and others
- HEMM (Heavy Earth Moving Machinery) Simulators — for mining operations
  including Tippers, Dozers, Dumpers, Wheel Loaders, LHDs, Shovels,
  Backhoes, and Excavators

CLOUD, DIGITAL, AND FEASIBILITY:
- Cloud Based Simulation Training — DNV Approved; 75,000+ certificates generated
- Automated Scenario / Simulation Based Assessment (ASBA)
- VR / AR / MR Based Simulator Training
- Hybrid / Blended Learning Capability
- SAFEPORT — Port and Waterway Navigation Feasibility Programme

3.3 KEY HIGHLIGHTS OF THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE

DNV Class A Certified:
The entire ARI marine and offshore simulation range holds DNV Class A
certification — the highest grade awarded by Det Norske Veritas for maritime
training simulators. This makes ARI one of the most comprehensively certified
simulation providers in the world.

Scalability:
Simulators are available in configurations ranging from low-cost desktop and
part-task trainers to large-scale full-mission installations with 360-degree
fields of view, motion platforms, and complete replica bridge or engine room
environments.

Integration:
Individual simulators can be networked and integrated into multi-simulator
complexes, enabling combined training across bridge, engine room, cargo, VTS,
tug, and communications stations simultaneously.

Green and Sustainable Training:
ARI's engine room simulators include the latest green fuel and decarbonisation
engine models (LNG, methanol, dual fuel) and emission control systems (HP SCR,
LP SCR, EGR), enabling training institutions to prepare seafarers for the
evolving demands of sustainable shipping.

Cloud and Remote Learning:
ARI introduced one of the first and largest cloud-based simulation systems for
maritime training. All simulator types are available via the cloud platform.
ARI is among only three globally certified companies approved by both IADC and
IWCF for cloud-based drilling and well control assessment.

Port Feasibility:
The SAFEPORT programme provides simulation-based feasibility studies for port
and waterway development projects, analysing vessel manoeuvring, tug requirements,
berth approaches, and river navigation safety before infrastructure is committed.

3.4 WHO IS THE MARITIME SIMULATION SUITE FOR?

- Maritime academies and training centres seeking IMO / STCW 2010 compliant
  simulator training and certification programmes
- Shipping companies and operators requiring crew training and competency
  assessment
- Offshore operators and oil and gas companies requiring DP, ROV, and
  offshore bridge training
- Port authorities and VTS centres requiring vessel traffic management training
- Port and harbour developers requiring pre-construction feasibility studies
- Classification societies and certification bodies requiring accredited
  simulation-based assessment tools

SECTION 4: SUITE 2 — THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 WHAT IS THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE?

The ARI Defence Simulation Suite (also referred to as the Naval Simulation Suite)
is a collection of sophisticated, high-fidelity training platforms built to
military standards. Each simulator in the suite provides a complete learning
experience for specific naval warfare, engineering, or mission operations.

The suite is designed for navies, armed forces, defence training establishments,
and national maritime security agencies. It covers the full spectrum of naval
training needs: from bridge operations and machinery management to submarine
warfare, cyber defence, and joint multi-force operations.

ARI's Naval Simulation Suite is considered one of the most powerful naval
simulation platforms available globally. The simulators have been delivered to
a significant number of armed forces worldwide.

The suite is built on the following core principles:

Integration and Interoperability:
Simulators are designed and built to deliver a high degree of integration and
interoperability. Multiple simulators and simulator types can be interfaced using
Common Databases and High Level Architecture (HLA) systems to permit platform,
fleet, and force-level training scenarios. The distributed simulation
infrastructure is fully compatible with secured defence-grade networks.

Flexibility:
Each simulator can be operated individually, or multiple simulators can be
combined into different configurations to create team training, whole-ship
training, or multiple collective whole-ship training platforms.

Mission Readiness:
From operations to tactical and strategic evaluation, from electronic warfare to
troop movement planning, the simulators can be deployed in a variety of roles.
Participants can visualise tactical inputs, analyse multiple courses of action,
and evaluate a range of decision response scenarios in combat or mobilisation
environments.

4.2 WHAT DOES THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE INCLUDE?

WARSHIP BRIDGE AND NAVIGATION:
- Bridge Operations Simulator (Primary and Secondary Warship Bridge) — full
  recreation of naval vessel bridge with military band Radars, EWS systems,
  weapon control, encrypted communications, and subsea threat tools
- ECDIS and RADAR/ARPA Room — IMO / STCW 2010 compliant navigation systems

SURFACE WARFARE:
- Fast Attack Craft (FAC) Simulator — 360-degree HFOV, 6-DOF motion platform,
  MMG simulation with recoil kit, weapon system integration
- Action Speed Tactical Trainer (ASTT) — multi-domain naval tactical training
  for ships, submarines, and aircraft command teams; supports ASW, AAW, EW,
  Mine, Amphibious, and Surface Warfare

COMBAT AND COMMAND:
- Combat Information Centre (CIC) Simulator — Surface Radar Modules, Sonar
  Module, Electronic Warfare Suite, and Fire Control System (FCS)

SUBSURFACE WARFARE:
- Submarine Operations and Navigation Simulator — navigation, PMS, periscope
  simulation, scenario-based training, crew coordination
- Deep Submergence Rescue Vehicle (DSRV) Simulator — hydrodynamic modelling,
  SRV and LARS station, ROV station

ENGINEERING AND PROPULSION:
- Warship Machinery Operations Simulator — high-speed engines, turbines,
  waterjet propulsors, fault injection, instructor control tools

AVIATION:
- Helicopter Control Simulation — ship-helicopter coordination, SCA, SAR,
  ASW, deck operations, weapons delivery

DOMAIN AWARENESS AND SECURITY:
- Domain Awareness and Coastal Security Solution — unified, real-time
  multi-domain naval surveillance and command solution
- Cyber Warfare Simulator — models cyber threats across Radar, Navigation,
  Sonar, EW, Communications, Vessel Control, Combat Systems, and Port
  Infrastructure

EMERGING TECHNOLOGY:
- Advanced Mixed Reality (MR) Solutions for Defence Training — combat and
  tactical training, naval operations simulation
- ROV / Tele-Robotic and Autonomous Systems Simulator — IED handling,
  urban and rural terrain, assessment scoring

CLOUD AND DIGITAL:
- Cloud-Based Simulation (secure, scalable, ASBA assessment)

INTEGRATED NAVAL SIMULATION COMPLEX:
- ARI Naval Simulation Complex — multi-simulator, HLA-enabled, unified
  control centre integrating all of the above simulators into a single
  training ecosystem

4.3 KEY HIGHLIGHTS OF THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE

Built to Military Standards:
All defence products are built to military standards and incorporate a
considerable set of unique defence-related features not found in commercial
maritime simulation. They have been delivered to and are trusted by multiple
armed forces around the world.

Full-Spectrum Naval Coverage:
The suite covers every domain of modern naval warfare — surface, subsurface,
air, electronic, and cyber — enabling holistic naval training from a single
integrated platform.

Integrated Multi-Simulator Complex:
The ARI Naval Simulation Complex integrates up to 13 individual simulators
(Primary and Secondary Warship Bridge, FAC, GMDSS, Machinery, Submarine,
DSRV, ECDIS/RADAR Room, ASTT, CIC, Helicopter Control, Coastal Security
Solution, and Unified Control Centre) into one unified training ecosystem
with real-time data exchange, dynamic threat modelling, and synchronised
operations.

Recording and Debriefing:
All simulators record complete training sessions including equipment operation
sequences, visual images, sensor data, CCTV views, and voice communications.
Recordings are fully synchronised, time-stamped, and can be replayed at variable
speeds, including live resumption from within a recording.

Cyber Domain Training:
The Cyber Warfare Simulator is one of the most comprehensive cyber-maritime
training platforms available, modelling attacks and defensive operations across
Radar, Navigation, Sonar, Electronic Warfare, Communications, Vessel Control
Systems, Combat and Weapon Systems, and Port Infrastructure.

Customisation:
ARI provides completely customised simulation solutions for specific vessels and
platforms, including their exact performance and handling footprints. Assets,
equipment, standard operating procedures, environment, and physical modelling
parameters can all be customised.

4.4 WHO IS THE DEFENCE SIMULATION SUITE FOR?

- Navies and naval training commands requiring high-fidelity simulation for
  individual and collective naval warfare training
- Armed forces and defence establishments requiring tactical, strategic, and
  mission rehearsal simulation platforms
- Submarine commands requiring training for navigation, sonar, propulsion, and
  emergency procedures
- Defence aviation units requiring ship-helicopter coordination training
- Coastal and maritime security agencies requiring domain awareness and
  surveillance training
- Cyber defence units requiring training in defending naval networks and
  shipborne systems against cyber threats

SECTION 5: HOW THE TWO SUITES RELATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARI Simulation's two suites — the Maritime Simulation Suite and the Defence
Simulation Suite — share a common technology foundation and many core simulation
components, while serving distinctly different operational purposes.

SHARED TECHNOLOGY FOUNDATION:
- Both suites are built on ARI's proven, certified simulation technology
- Both use state-of-the-art real-time visualisation systems
- Both support multi-simulator integration and interoperability
- Both include ECDIS, RADAR/ARPA, GMDSS, and bridge simulation components
  (adapted respectively for civilian and military use)
- Both offer cloud-based and mixed reality training options

KEY DIFFERENCES:

Maritime Simulation Suite:
- Designed for commercial maritime operators, training institutions, and
  certification bodies
- Focus on IMO / STCW 2010 compliance and DNV Class A certification
- Covers cargo operations, offshore operations, port and crane training,
  and feasibility studies
- Optimised for seafarer training, certification, and competency assessment

Defence Simulation Suite:
- Designed for navies and armed forces
- Built to military standards with unique defence-related features
- Covers naval warfare, tactical operations, cyber defence, and joint forces
  training
- Optimised for mission rehearsal, tactical proving, combat readiness, and
  operational training

Together, the two suites position ARI Simulation as one of the most
comprehensive simulation and training solution providers in the world,
covering Land, Sea, and Air training needs across both commercial and
defence sectors.

SECTION 7: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What are the two main simulation suites ARI offers?
A: ARI offers the Maritime Simulation Suite (for commercial maritime, offshore,
and port operators) and the Defence Simulation Suite (for navies and armed forces
worldwide). Both suites are built on ARI's proven simulation technology and share
a common core platform, but are adapted for their respective sectors and standards.

Q: Is ARI's simulation technology the same for both maritime and defence?
A: Yes, both suites are built on ARI's proven and certified simulation technology.
Defence products additionally incorporate a considerable set of unique
defence-related features and are built to military standards, while the maritime
suite is optimised for IMO, STCW 2010, and DNV Class A compliance.

Q: What does the Maritime Simulation Suite cover?
A: The Maritime Simulation Suite covers the full range of commercial marine and
offshore training needs. It includes Navigation and Bridge, Engine Room, Cargo
and Process Handling, Offshore (DP, Anchor Handling, ROV), Ports and Terminals
(Crane and HEMM), Cloud and Digital Technologies (ASBA, VR/MR), and Port
Feasibility Studies (SAFEPORT). The entire marine and offshore range is DNV
Class A certified.

Q: What does the Defence Simulation Suite cover?
A: The Defence Simulation Suite covers naval bridge operations, fast attack craft
simulation, action speed tactical training, combat information centres, submarine
operations, deep submergence rescue, warship machinery, helicopter control,
domain awareness and coastal security, cyber warfare, and mixed reality training.
All simulators can be integrated into a single Naval Simulation Complex for
joint multi-force training.

Q: Who are the customers for each suite?
A: The Maritime Suite serves maritime academies, training centres, shipping
companies, offshore operators, port authorities, and certification bodies. The
Defence Suite serves navies, armed forces, naval training commands, submarine
commands, defence aviation units, and maritime security agencies.

Q: Can ARI simulators from both suites work together?
A: Yes. ARI uses Common Databases and High Level Architecture (HLA) systems to
achieve interoperability between simulators. The distributed simulation
infrastructure is compatible with secured defence-grade networks, and ARI has
delivered integrated multi-simulator complexes that combine multiple simulator
types into unified training ecosystems.

Q: How widely are ARI simulators used?
A: ARI's simulators are installed in over 60 countries with more than 1,500
installations worldwide. The Defence Suite has been delivered to a significant
number of global armed forces. The Maritime Suite has generated over 75,000
certificates through ARI's cloud simulation systems.

Q: Does ARI offer cloud-based training for both suites?
A: Yes. Cloud-based training is available across ARI's portfolio for both
suites. The marine range of cloud modules is DNV Approved. ARI is among only
three globally certified simulation companies approved by both IADC and IWCF
for cloud-based drilling and well control assessment.

Q: Does ARI offer customised simulation solutions?
A: Yes. Customisation is a core strength of ARI. ARI has designed and delivered
a large number of customised solutions for both suites, from fully custom-built
vessel simulations to complex multi-trainee and mission-specific platforms.
Assets, equipment, standard operating procedures, environment, and physical
modelling can all be customised.
