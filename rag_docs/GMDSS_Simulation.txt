SECTION 2: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ARI Communications Simulator provides complete operational communication
training using shipboard equipment. It is built to recreate the conditions and
equipment found aboard modern vessels, giving trainees an authentic, hands-on
experience without requiring access to a real ship.

The simulator complies with STCW simulator performance standards and meets DNV
Class A requirements. AMERC-approved configurations are available for institutions
requiring official certification pathways. It recreates modern communication
station operations for training personnel at all levels in distress and routine
maritime communications.

The ARI GMDSS Simulator is a critical component of maritime safety training,
focusing on the Global Maritime Distress and Safety System (GMDSS). It is designed
to provide a comprehensive operating environment that replicates a modern ship's
communication station, ensuring that navigators at all levels are proficient in
handling both routine and emergency communications.

The simulator is rigorously certified, meeting STCW 2010 requirements and DNV
Class A standards. This ensures that training completed on the system is globally
recognised and provides a certified pathway for maritime personnel to master the
equipment and protocols necessary for maintaining a vital communication link
between the vessel and shore-based rescue coordination centres.

SECTION 3: WHAT IS GMDSS?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

GMDSS stands for the Global Maritime Distress and Safety System. It is an
internationally agreed-upon set of safety procedures, types of equipment, and
communication protocols used to increase safety and make it easier to rescue
distressed ships, boats, and aircraft.

GMDSS is mandated by the International Maritime Organization (IMO) and is
required aboard all vessels over 300 gross tonnage on international voyages and
all passenger ships. It replaces the older Morse code-based distress system.

Key elements of GMDSS that the simulator covers include:
- Digital Selective Calling (DSC) on VHF, MF, and HF frequencies
- NAVTEX (Navigational Telex) for receiving maritime safety information
- Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacons (EPIRB)
- Search and Rescue Transponders (SART)
- Inmarsat satellite communications
- VHF radiotelephone communications
- MF/HF radiotelephone and radioteletype communications

SECTION 4: CERTIFICATIONS AND COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 STCW 2010 COMPLIANCE

The ARI GMDSS Simulator is fully compliant with the Standards of Training,
Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers (STCW) 2010 amendments.

Specific sections met:
- Section A-I/12: Mandatory minimum requirements for the use of simulators
- Section B-I/12: Guidance regarding the use of simulators

STCW compliance means the simulator meets internationally defined standards for
how simulation-based training must be conducted, assessed, and certified. Training
completed on an STCW-compliant simulator is recognised by maritime authorities
and flag states worldwide.

The Manila Amendments (STCW 2010) introduced updated requirements for simulator
performance standards. The ARI simulator was designed and certified to meet these
updated requirements in full.

4.2 DNV CLASS A STANDARD

The ARI GMDSS Simulator is certified to DNV Class A standard.

DNV (Det Norske Veritas) is a globally recognised classification society and
certification body. DNV Class A is the highest classification awarded for
maritime training simulators. It denotes that the simulator:
- Accurately replicates the equipment, environment, and operational conditions
  of the real system it represents
- Meets the most rigorous performance benchmarks for training fidelity
- Is suitable for certification-level training and competency assessment
- Has been independently audited and verified by DNV assessors

Class A certification distinguishes the simulator from lower-tier Class B and
Class C simulators, which are suitable only for familiarisation or limited
task training. Class A is required when simulation is used as the basis for
formal certification of seafarer competence.

4.3 AMERC APPROVAL

Specific configurations of the ARI GMDSS Simulator are approved by AMERC
(the Association of Marine Electronics and Radio Colleges).

AMERC is a UK-based organisation that approves training establishments and
equipment for the delivery of GMDSS radio operator courses and examinations.
AMERC approval is required for training providers who wish to offer GMDSS
Short Range Certificate (SRC), Long Range Certificate (LRC), and other
radio operator qualifications.

AMERC-approved configurations of the simulator can be used by training
institutions to deliver accredited GMDSS operator training and formal
certification examinations.

SECTION 5: KEY FEATURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 STCW 2010 COMPLIANCE
Meets all performance standards defined in STCW Sections A-I/12 and B-I/12,
ensuring that training delivered on the simulator satisfies international
mandatory minimum requirements for simulator-based maritime training.

5.2 DNV CLASS A STANDARD
Certified to the highest industry standard for simulation quality. DNV Class A
certification confirms the simulator accurately represents real shipboard
communication systems and is suitable for formal competency assessment and
certification-level training programmes.

5.3 AMERC APPROVAL
Specific configurations are approved by AMERC for official GMDSS radio operator
certification. This makes the simulator suitable for use in accredited training
establishments delivering GMDSS qualifications including SRC and LRC certificates.

5.4 FULL-SPECTRUM COMMUNICATIONS
The simulator covers both satellite and terrestrial communication systems,
providing trainees with exposure to the complete range of communication
technologies used aboard modern vessels. This includes:
- Satellite communications (Inmarsat systems)
- VHF radio (Very High Frequency)
- MF radio (Medium Frequency)
- HF radio (High Frequency)
- Digital Selective Calling (DSC)
- NAVTEX receivers
- EPIRB and SART equipment

5.5 DISTRESS HANDLING
Specialised training modules focus on managing emergency signals and
coordinating with rescue services. Trainees learn to:
- Initiate and respond to DSC distress alerts
- Communicate with Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres (MRCCs)
- Operate EPIRBs and SARTs correctly
- Follow GMDSS distress communication protocols
- Handle distress, urgency, and safety calls on appropriate frequencies

5.6 SYMMETRIC ENVIRONMENT
The simulator replicates the actual GMDSS communication station found on
modern commercial ships. This symmetric design means trainees interact with
realistic representations of the equipment they will use at sea, reducing
the gap between simulator training and real-world operations. The environment
includes faithful recreations of control panels, displays, and communication
interfaces as found aboard operational vessels.

SECTION 6: TRAINING CAPABILITIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 TARGET AUDIENCE
The ARI GMDSS Simulator is designed to train maritime personnel at all levels,
including:
- Officer cadets and navigation students in initial training
- Junior officers working towards GMDSS certificates
- Senior officers refreshing or updating their qualifications
- Radio operators and communication officers
- Maritime instructors and assessors

6.2 ROUTINE COMMUNICATIONS TRAINING
Trainees develop proficiency in day-to-day maritime communication tasks:
- Port authority and vessel traffic service (VTS) communications
- Ship-to-ship communications
- Routine position reporting
- Weather information reception via NAVTEX
- Satellite communication for voice and data

6.3 EMERGENCY AND DISTRESS COMMUNICATIONS TRAINING
Trainees are trained to respond correctly under pressure in emergency scenarios:
- Sending and acknowledging DSC distress alerts on VHF Channel 70, MF 2187.5 kHz,
  and HF frequencies
- Voice distress calls on VHF Channel 16
- Coordinating with Search and Rescue (SAR) services
- Activating and registering EPIRBs
- Operating SARTs to assist rescuers in locating a vessel
- Inter-ship coordination during distress events

6.4 SCENARIO-BASED TRAINING
The simulator supports a range of configurable scenarios allowing instructors to:
- Simulate realistic distress situations (fire, flooding, collision, man overboard)
- Introduce multiple simultaneous communications tasks
- Vary the complexity and urgency of scenarios to match trainee level
- Assess trainee performance against STCW competency benchmarks

SECTION 7: TECHNICAL ENVIRONMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The simulator replicates a complete modern GMDSS ship station, including:

Communication Equipment Replicated:
- VHF DSC Controller and radiotelephone
- MF/HF DSC Controller and radiotelephone
- Inmarsat-C terminal (for data and distress messaging)
- Inmarsat Fleet (voice satellite communications)
- NAVTEX receiver
- EPIRB (manual and float-free activation simulation)
- Two-way VHF portable radiotelephones
- SART (Search and Rescue Transponder) operation

The environment is designed to be symmetric — meaning the layout, controls,
and interfaces match those found on real vessels, so the transition from
simulator to shipboard operation is as seamless as possible.

SECTION 10: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What certification standard does the ARI GMDSS Simulator meet?
A: The ARI GMDSS Simulator meets STCW 2010 performance standards (Sections
A-I/12 and B-I/12) and is certified to DNV Class A, the highest grade for
maritime training simulators. Specific configurations also hold AMERC approval.

Q: Is this simulator suitable for formal GMDSS certification courses?
A: Yes. The DNV Class A certification and AMERC approval make it suitable for
delivering formal GMDSS operator certification courses, including SRC and LRC
qualifications at accredited training establishments.

Q: What communication systems does the simulator cover?
A: The simulator covers full-spectrum communications including VHF, MF, HF,
satellite communications (Inmarsat), DSC, NAVTEX, EPIRB, and SART — all systems
found in a complete modern ship GMDSS station.

Q: What level of trainee is the simulator designed for?
A: The simulator is designed for personnel at all levels, from officer cadets
in initial training through to experienced officers undertaking refresher or
revalidation training.

Q: What is the difference between DNV Class A and lower-class simulators?
A: DNV Class A is the highest classification for maritime simulators and is
required when simulation is used for formal certification of competence. Class B
and C simulators are suitable for familiarisation or limited-task training only
and cannot be used as the sole basis for certification assessment.

Q: What does AMERC approval mean for training providers?
A: AMERC approval means the specific simulator configuration has been assessed
and authorised by the Association of Marine Electronics and Radio Colleges for
use in delivering accredited GMDSS radio operator courses and examinations in
the UK and internationally recognised programmes.

Q: Does the simulator replicate real shipboard equipment?
A: Yes. The symmetric environment design means the simulator faithfully
replicates the layout, controls, and interfaces of a real shipboard GMDSS
communication station, minimising the gap between simulator training and
actual sea-going operations.
