SECTION 3: WHAT IS RADAR AND ARPA?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 RADAR (Radio Detecting And Ranging)

RADAR is an electronic navigation system used aboard ships to detect the
presence, range, bearing, and movement of other vessels, landmasses, and
navigational hazards. It operates by transmitting radio waves and analysing
the echoes returned from objects in the surrounding environment.

At sea, radar is a primary tool for:
- Collision avoidance in restricted visibility (fog, rain, night)
- Coastal and harbour navigation
- Monitoring vessel traffic in busy waterways
- Detecting navigational marks and fixed hazards

3.2 ARPA (Automatic Radar Plotting Aid)

ARPA is a computerised system integrated with radar that automatically tracks
targets detected on the radar display. It calculates and continuously updates
each target's course, speed, closest point of approach (CPA), and time to
closest point of approach (TCPA).

ARPA provides navigators with:
- Automated tracking of multiple targets simultaneously
- Collision threat assessment for each tracked target
- Trial manoeuvre capability to evaluate avoidance actions before execution
- Reduced workload and improved situational awareness compared to manual plotting

Both RADAR and ARPA proficiency are mandatory under the STCW Convention for
officers in charge of a navigational watch.

SECTION 4: CERTIFICATIONS AND COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 IMO PERFORMANCE STANDARDS COMPLIANCE

The ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator is fully compliant with IMO (International Maritime
Organization) performance standards for radar equipment on seagoing vessels.

IMO performance standards define the minimum technical and operational
requirements that radar and ARPA equipment must meet when fitted aboard ships
subject to the SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea) Convention. By building the
simulator to these standards, the training environment accurately reflects the
behaviour, capabilities, and limitations of compliant shipboard equipment.

Compliance with IMO performance standards ensures:
- Training fidelity matches real operational equipment
- Competencies developed in the simulator are directly transferable to the vessel
- The simulator is accepted by maritime administrations as a valid training tool

4.2 STCW 2010 COMPLIANCE

The ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator meets STCW 2010 requirements for simulator-based
training and assessment.

The Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers (STCW)
Convention, as amended by the Manila Amendments of 2010, mandates specific
competency standards for navigational officers. Radar and ARPA operation are
core competencies under STCW Table A-II/1 (officer in charge of a navigational
watch) and related tables.

STCW 2010 compliance means:
- Training delivered on the simulator satisfies mandatory international requirements
- Certificates of proficiency issued following simulator-based training are
  globally recognised by flag states and port state control authorities
- The simulator supports both initial certification and revalidation training

4.3 IMO MODEL COURSE 1.07 — RADAR NAVIGATION AT OPERATIONAL LEVEL

The simulator is designed to directly support IMO Model Course 1.07: Radar
Navigation, Radar Plotting and Use of ARPA at the Operational Level.

Model Course 1.07 covers:
- Basic radar operation and interpretation
- Manual plotting of radar targets
- Understanding radar limitations and sources of error
- Use of radar in collision avoidance in compliance with COLREGs
- Operational-level ARPA use including target acquisition and tracking

This course is aimed at officers of the watch and deck officers at the
operational level of responsibility.

4.4 IMO MODEL COURSE 1.08 — RADAR NAVIGATION AT MANAGEMENT LEVEL

The simulator also supports IMO Model Course 1.08: Radar Navigation, Radar
Plotting and Use of ARPA at the Management Level.

Model Course 1.08 covers:
- Advanced radar and ARPA operation
- Complex collision avoidance scenarios involving multiple vessels
- Evaluation and supervision of watchkeeping personnel using radar
- Use of radar as a primary tool for navigational decision-making at the
  management level of responsibility

This course is aimed at masters, chief mates, and officers at the management
level of responsibility.

SECTION 6: TRAINING CAPABILITIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 TARGET AUDIENCE
The ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator is designed for naval and marine personnel at
all levels, including:
- Officer cadets and navigation students undertaking initial radar training
- Officers of the watch seeking STCW radar certification at operational level
- Senior officers and masters undertaking management-level radar certification
- Personnel undertaking STCW revalidation and refresher training
- Maritime instructors and assessors delivering accredited radar courses

6.2 OPERATIONAL-LEVEL TRAINING (IMO MODEL COURSE 1.07)
Trainees develop foundational and operational radar competencies:
- Correct operation and interpretation of radar displays
- Understanding of radar limitations including blind sectors, sea and rain clutter
- Manual and ARPA-assisted target plotting
- Collision avoidance using radar in compliance with COLREGs
- Use of parallel index lines for coastal navigation
- Radar navigation in restricted visibility

6.3 MANAGEMENT-LEVEL TRAINING (IMO MODEL COURSE 1.08)
Trainees develop advanced radar competencies for management-level responsibility:
- Complex multi-vessel collision avoidance scenarios
- Assessment and supervision of watchkeeping officer radar competency
- Advanced ARPA interpretation and decision-making under high traffic density
- Integration of radar with ECDIS and other navigational systems
- Radar use in pilotage waters and restricted channels

6.4 SCENARIO-BASED TRAINING
Instructors can configure scenarios to include:
- Varying weather and visibility conditions (fog, rain, restricted visibility)
- High traffic density environments (TSS, port approaches, straits)
- Multiple simultaneous collision threats requiring prioritised responses
- Coastal and open ocean navigation challenges
- Night-time and dawn/dusk scenarios

6.5 COLLISION AVOIDANCE TRAINING
A core focus of the simulator is developing proficiency in collision avoidance
in compliance with the International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at
Sea (COLREGs). Trainees learn to:
- Assess collision risk using CPA and TCPA data
- Determine the correct action required by COLREGs rules
- Execute and monitor collision avoidance manoeuvres
- Resume course after a collision avoidance situation has been resolved

SECTION 7: TECHNICAL ENVIRONMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 EQUIPMENT CONFIGURATION OPTIONS

Real OEM Equipment:
The simulator can be fitted with genuine radar hardware from original equipment
manufacturers. This provides the highest level of operational realism, as
trainees interact with the exact controls, displays, and interfaces they will
use aboard the vessels they serve on.

Emulated Radar Systems:
High-fidelity emulated radar sets replicate the look, feel, and behaviour of
real radar systems in software. Emulated systems allow training institutions to
represent multiple radar types and manufacturers within a single training
environment, increasing versatility and cost-effectiveness.

7.2 ARPA FUNCTIONALITY REPLICATED
- Automatic and manual target acquisition
- Multi-target tracking (simultaneous tracking of multiple contacts)
- Continuous calculation and display of CPA and TCPA
- Vector display (true and relative motion modes)
- Trial manoeuvre function
- Guard zones and collision alarm generation
- Target data readout (course, speed, CPA, TCPA, bearing, range)

7.3 RADAR DISPLAY MODES
- True motion and relative motion display
- North-up, course-up, and head-up orientation
- Adjustable range scales
- Electronic Bearing Lines (EBL) and Variable Range Markers (VRM)
- Parallel Index (PI) lines for coastal navigation

SECTION 10: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What standards does the ARI RADAR/ARPA Simulator comply with?
A: The simulator is fully compliant with IMO performance standards for radar
equipment on seagoing vessels and meets STCW 2010 requirements for simulator-
based training and assessment. It directly supports IMO Model Courses 1.07
and 1.08.

Q: What is the difference between Model Course 1.07 and 1.08?
A: IMO Model Course 1.07 covers radar navigation and ARPA use at the operational
level, aimed at officers of the watch. IMO Model Course 1.08 covers the same
subjects at the management level, aimed at masters and chief mates who must
demonstrate advanced competency and the ability to supervise others.

Q: Can the simulator be configured with our existing radar brand?
A: Yes. The simulator supports configuration with real OEM radar equipment from
original manufacturers, allowing training institutions to match the specific
radar systems used by their trainees' employers. High-fidelity emulated radar
sets are also available as an alternative.

Q: Is the simulator suitable for STCW revalidation training?
A: Yes. The simulator meets STCW 2010 requirements for simulator-based training
and assessment, making it suitable for both initial certification and periodic
revalidation of radar and ARPA competencies.

Q: What collision avoidance capabilities does the simulator include?
A: The simulator provides full ARPA plotting with automatic and manual target
acquisition, continuous CPA and TCPA calculation, trial manoeuvre functionality,
guard zones, and collision alarm generation. Scenarios can be configured with
multiple simultaneous collision threats in compliance with COLREGs training
requirements.

Q: Does the simulator support both OEM and emulated radar systems?
A: Yes. Training institutions can choose to equip the simulator with genuine
OEM radar hardware for maximum realism, or deploy high-fidelity emulated radar
sets for greater flexibility and cost-effectiveness. Both options deliver
IMO-compliant training outcomes.

Q: What weather and environmental conditions can be simulated?
A: The simulator supports a wide range of conditions including fog, rain, and
restricted visibility, high-density traffic scenarios, port approaches, traffic
separation schemes, coastal and open ocean environments, and day, night, and
dawn/dusk lighting conditions.

Q: Who is the simulator designed to train?
A: The simulator is designed for naval and marine personnel at all levels,
from officer cadets in initial radar training through to masters and chief mates
undertaking management-level certification or revalidation.
